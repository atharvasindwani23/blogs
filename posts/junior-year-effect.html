Junior Year Effect

I've seen it all in my junior year of college. Like, literally EVERYTHING.

Back in freshman year, I told my roommate that there exists a time-warp spell called "The Junior Year Effect" — the year where you see the most happen in the least time.

And well, it turned out to be real.

I'm not sure why this happens, but I know it WILL keep happening until the sun engulfs the earth.

It's the year where I found my core friends. (In my case I got lucky — amazing roommates and a very talent-dense group of g's.)

It's the year where I found people who aren't so great.

It's the year I realized how unfit I was and decided to make a change.

It's the year where I got my first proper internship.

It's the year where I missed my parents the most.

It's the year that challenged me the most.

It's the year in which I grew the most.

So here's the advice I'd give to whoever's reading this:

If you haven't had your junior year yet — make the most of it.

If you've already had it — try to replicate it.

Try to have a year, one in every three, where you genuinely push yourself. It can be for anything. Have a year where you ask yourself uncomfortable questions that lead to more uncomfortable decisions. Have a year where you find yourself again.
